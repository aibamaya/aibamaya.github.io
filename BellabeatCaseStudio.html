Bellabeat Case Study on Kaggle

Welcome to the Bellabeat data analysis case study!
This case study analyzes smart device ﬁtness data to help unlock new growth opportunities for the Bellabeat company. The insights discovered will help guide a marketing strategy for the company.

About the Company

Bellabeat is a high-tech company that manufactures health-focused smart products including, the Bellabeat app, Leaf, Time, and Spring. Collecting data on activity, sleep, stress, and reproductive health has allowed Bellabeat to empower women with knowledge about their health and habits. Bellabeat also offers a subscription-based membership program for users, giving them access to personalized guidance on having a healthy lifestyle. All of this has positioned itself as a tech-driven wellness company for women.

Characters:

  • Urška Sršen: Bellabeat’s co-founder and Chief Creative Oﬃcer.
  • Sando Mur: Mathematician and Bellabeat co-founder.
  • Marketing Analytics team: A team of data analysts responsible for collecting, analyzing, and reporting data that helps guide Bellabeat’s marketing strategy

Business Task

Analyze smart device usage of an existing competitor to identify how consumers use non-Bellabeat devices. Then identify potential opportunities for growth and recommendations for the Bellabeat marketing strategy improvement based on the insights of that analysis.

Questions for the Analysis:

  • What are some trends in smart device usage?
  • How could these trends apply to Bellabeat customers?
  • How could these trends help inﬂuence Bellabeat marketing strategy?

Data Selection

The data for this analysis is the Fitbit Fitness Tracker Data stored on Kaggle and made available by Mobius. This dataset is under CC0: Public Domain license meaning the creator has waived his right to the work under copyright law

Data Organization and Description:

This data set contains a personal ﬁtness tracker from thirty ﬁtbit users. It includes information about daily activity, steps, and heart rate that can be used to explore users’ habits. It was generated via a survey through Amazon Mechanical Turk between 12.03.2016 and 12.05.2016. Thirty eligible Fitbit users consented to submit their tracker data, including minute-level output for physical activity, heart rate, and sleep monitoring. Variation between output represents different Fitbit trackers and individual tracking behaviors/preferences.

Limitations of this Data

  • Sample size: the dataset collects the information from 30 individuals, which is a small sample size to determine behavior patterns of a population of people who use smart devices
  • The absence of essential characteristics of the participants might have a significant impact on the data collected, such as gender, age, and lifestyle
  • Third-party data collected using Amazon Mechanical Turk.
  • The data was collected six years ago. It is not as current as it could be for the analysis.

Insights of the Summarization

  • This summary shows the average user is taking 7638 steps a day. It appears that healthy adults can take anywhere between 4,000 and 18,000 steps/day and that 10,000 steps/day is a reasonable target for healthy adults.
  • Users get 21.16 minutes of very active activity in a day or 148.12 minutes in a week. The CDC recommends at least 75–150 minutes of vigorous-intensity aerobic or an equivalent combination of moderate- and vigorous-intensity activity throughout the week. May increase moderate-intensity aerobic physical activity to more than 300 minutes or do more than 150 minutes of vigorous-intensity aerobic or an equivalent combination of moderate- and vigorous-intensity activity throughout the week for additional health benefits.
  • Participants are averaging 991.2 minutes, or 16.52 hours of sedentary time a day. Scientists have determined that should limit the amount of time spent being sedentary. Replacing sedentary time with physical activity of any intensity provides health benefits. And to help reduce the detrimental effects of high levels of sedentary behavior on health, all adults and older adults should aim to do more than the recommended levels of moderate- to vigorous-intensity physical activity.
  • This summary shows the average user is burning 2304 calories a day. Studies show that the number of calories you should burn in a day depends on your health and fitness goals, as well as other factors like your age, sex, height, weight, and activity levels. But on average, males and females need roughly 2,200–3,000 and 1,600–2,200 calories per day, respectively.
  • The summary of the sleep data shows the average user sleeps once per day for 419.2 minutes or 7 hours. That falls within the CDC’s recommendations for adults to get the proper amount of rest. The average user is spending 458.6 minutes in bed, or 7.6 hours. That means the typical user is spending 39.4 minutes awake in bed.

Recommendations

After analyzing FitBit Fitness Tracker Data is possible to use these insights to help influence Bellabeat marketing strategy.

  • Bellabeat app can include alerts to warn the users when they accumulate a high number of sedentary minutes. Also, the Bellabeat app can make suggestions of activities to help the user to get more active.
  • Encourage people to be more active, and since the most active time happens from 17-19, the Bellabeat app can set reminders to encourage people to train at those hours to increase.
  • Encourage women to reach the goal of 10.000 steps per day. The Bellabeat app can set reminders of how many steps are missing to get 10.000 and motivate them with the benefits that reaching that goal implies to their health.
  • Since Saturdays seem to be the more active day of the week, remind people to walk more long distances, train more time, or be more active in general this day.
  • Sundays tend to be the most sedentary day of the week; because of this, the Bellabeat app can use this day to motivate people to do relaxing routines to be more active to begin the week. And guide them to their webpage, where they can learn how to construct a relaxing day.
  • Bellabeat can also help women improve the quality of their sleep. A reminder can be set one hour before the set bedtime to help them get ready for bed.
  • Encourage women who sleep less than six hours to be more active or reduce sedentary time during the day to help them sleep more hours and improve their sleep quality.
  • Also, consider that more data is needed to support these insights because the correlation between some data does not mean causation. And invest in data which greater sample size and more data from participants like age and gender to have more accurate predictions.
